# Inspira DMCA Policy

**Effective date:** 2026-04-24
**Last updated:** 2026-04-20

Inspira respects the intellectual property rights of others and expects users of the Service to do the same. This policy explains how copyright owners can request the removal of material they believe infringes their rights, and how users whose material has been removed may respond. It is written with reference to the United States Digital Millennium Copyright Act ("DMCA"), 17 U.S.C. § 512.

## 2. Submitting a notice of claimed infringement

To be effective under the DMCA, a notice must be in writing and include substantially the following:

1. **Identification of the copyrighted work** claimed to have been infringed, or a representative list of works if the notice covers multiple items.
2. **Identification of the material that is claimed to be infringing** and information reasonably sufficient to let us locate it — for example, a direct URL or project and topic identifier inside Inspira.
3. **Your contact information** — full legal name, mailing address, telephone number, and email address.
4. **A statement** that you have a good-faith belief that the use of the material in the manner complained of is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.
5. **A statement**, under penalty of perjury, that the information in the notice is accurate and that you are the copyright owner or are authorized to act on behalf of the owner of an exclusive right that is allegedly infringed.
6. **A physical or electronic signature** of a person authorized to act on behalf of the owner.

Send the notice to **dmca@tryinspira.com** (preferred) or by postal mail to the address above.

**Misrepresentation warning.** Under 17 U.S.C. § 512(f), any person who knowingly materially misrepresents that material is infringing, or that material or activity was removed or disabled by mistake, may be liable for damages, including costs and attorneys' fees. Only submit a notice if you believe in good faith that the use is not authorized.

## 3. What happens after we receive a valid notice

On receipt of a notice that substantially complies with Section 2, we will:

1. Remove or disable access to the allegedly infringing material.
2. Take reasonable steps to notify the user who posted it that the material has been removed or disabled.
3. Forward the notice (or the relevant portions of it, with personal data redacted where law permits) to the user.
4. Record the event in our internal repeat-infringer log (Section 6).

If the notice does not substantially comply with Section 2, we may respond with a request for additional information before acting, or may decline to act.

We do not adjudicate copyright disputes. Our role under the DMCA is to facilitate the notice, removal, and counter-notice process.

## 4. Counter-notice procedure

If your content was removed or disabled as a result of a DMCA notice and you believe the removal was a mistake or misidentification, you may submit a counter-notice.

A counter-notice must include substantially the following:

1. **Your physical or electronic signature.**
2. **Identification of the material** that was removed or disabled and the location at which it appeared before removal.
3. **A statement, under penalty of perjury**, that you have a good-faith belief the material was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification of the material.
4. **Your full legal name, mailing address, and telephone number**, and a statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of the federal district court for the judicial district in which your address is located, or, if your address is outside the United States, for any judicial district in which Inspira may be found, and that you will accept service of process from the person who filed the notice under Section 2 or an agent of that person.

Send the counter-notice to **dmca@tryinspira.com** or to the mailing address above.

On receipt of a counter-notice that substantially complies with these requirements, we will promptly forward a copy to the person who filed the original notice. If that person does not file an action seeking a court order to restrain the alleged infringement within ten (10) to fourteen (14) business days, we may restore the material.

---

## 5. Caution before filing a counter-notice

Filing a counter-notice has legal consequences. It is a formal statement under penalty of perjury that subjects you to the jurisdiction of a federal court. If you are unsure whether the material was removed in error, consult an attorney before filing a counter-notice.

## 6. Repeat-infringer policy

We terminate, in appropriate circumstances, the Accounts of users who are repeat infringers. A user is considered a repeat infringer if, over a rolling 12-month period, they are the subject of two or more valid DMCA notices that are not successfully counter-noticed, or if they are determined by a court or by us, acting in good faith and in light of the facts available, to have repeatedly uploaded infringing material.

We maintain an internal log of DMCA notices, counter-notices, and enforcement actions. This log is retained for at least two years for purposes of enforcement and as required by law.

Termination may include immediate removal of Account access, deletion of content, and forfeiture of fees. See also our [Terms of Service](./terms-of-service.md) and [Acceptable Use Policy](./acceptable-use.md).

---

## 7. Notices and counter-notices are legal documents

Do not use these procedures to resolve ordinary content disputes, trademark disputes, privacy disputes, or claims of defamation. Those should be submitted through the channels described in our [Acceptable Use Policy](./acceptable-use.md) or our [Privacy Policy](./privacy-policy.md).

Claims based on laws other than U.S. copyright law (for example, moral-rights claims under some European jurisdictions, database-rights claims under EU law, or trademark and publicity-rights claims) are not handled under this DMCA procedure. Send those to **hello@tryinspira.com** with a clear statement of the applicable law and the basis for your claim, and we will evaluate them separately.
