# Inspira Acceptable Use Policy

**Effective date:** 2026-04-24
**Last updated:** 2026-04-20

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") describes the activities that are not permitted on Inspira ("the Service"). It is incorporated into our [Terms of Service](./terms-of-service.md) by reference and applies to every user of the Service. Violations can result in suspension or termination of your Account.

If you are unsure whether an intended use is permitted, email **hello@tryinspira.com** before acting.

---

## 1. Prohibited content

You may not use the Service to create, store, transmit, or generate content that:

- **is illegal** under the laws of the United States or of any jurisdiction where the content is created or accessed;
- **depicts or exploits children** — including child sexual abuse material ("CSAM"), grooming content, or any sexualized depiction of minors, whether real or generated. CSAM discovered on the Service is reported to the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children ("NCMEC") as required by 18 U.S.C. § 2258A and to equivalent authorities in other jurisdictions;
- **incites, promotes, or facilitates violence** against individuals or groups, including terrorism, mass harm, or targeted harassment campaigns;
- **promotes or enables self-harm** — including suicide instructions, pro-eating-disorder content, or content designed to encourage self-injury;
- **harasses, bullies, stalks, or defames** an identifiable individual or group;
- **infringes intellectual-property rights** — copyright, trademark, trade secret, patent, or moral rights. For copyright claims, see our [DMCA Policy](./dmca-policy.md);
- **violates privacy or publicity rights** — including posting a person's private information, government identifiers, home address, biometric data, or intimate images without consent;
- **contains malware** or exploits, or any code designed to damage, disable, overburden, or impair any system, service, or user;
- **facilitates unlawful surveillance** of individuals, including stalkerware targeting a specific person;
- **is designed to deceive** for malicious purposes, including phishing, impersonation, fraud, or scams;
- **is sexually explicit content involving non-consenting persons**, including non-consensual intimate imagery, voyeuristic content, and non-consensual deepfakes;
- **advocates hate** based on race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, caste, disability, age, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, or veteran status.

---

## 2. Prohibited activities

You may not use the Service to:

- **send spam, bulk unsolicited communications, or automated messaging** of any kind not expressly authorized by the recipient and by us;
- **scrape, crawl, or programmatically harvest** data from the Service except through documented APIs and within documented rate limits;
- **resell, sublicense, or repackage** Inspira or substantial portions of its output as a stand-alone product or feature offered to third parties, unless we have agreed in writing;
- **reverse engineer, decompile, disassemble, or otherwise attempt to derive the source code** of the Service, except to the minimum extent permitted by applicable law (for example, interoperability studies in the EU under Directive 2009/24/EC);
- **use outputs of the Service to train, fine-tune, evaluate, or benchmark** any machine-learning model that competes with Inspira or with our third-party AI providers;
- **impersonate** any person or organization, or misrepresent your affiliation with any person or organization;
- **forge, falsify, or alter** identifiers, headers, or metadata to conceal the origin of content;
- **distribute disinformation at scale** — including AI-generated political disinformation, synthetic media campaigns, election-interference material, or coordinated inauthentic behavior intended to manipulate public opinion;
- **harvest personally identifiable information ("PII")** — including using the Service to build, extend, or enrich a database of personal data about individuals who have not consented;
- **conduct credential stuffing, account takeover, or other automated authentication attacks** against the Service or any other service;
- **circumvent access controls, usage limits, security measures, or geoblocks** in the Service;
- **overload the Service or any third party** through high-volume traffic, runaway automation, or denial-of-service attacks;
- **conceal Account ownership or engage in Account trafficking** — selling, transferring, or purchasing Accounts without our consent;
- **facilitate regulated activities** — gambling, firearms, prescription pharmaceuticals, MLM / pyramid schemes — in ways that violate applicable law.

---

## 3. AI-specific restrictions

Because Inspira relies on third-party large language models, the following additional restrictions apply. You may not:

- use the Service to generate content intended to deceive viewers into believing a real person said or did something they did not, without clear disclosure and lawful consent;
- use AI Output to generate synthetic voices or likenesses of real people without their consent, except for commentary, parody, or other lawful fair use;
- attempt to manipulate the model through prompt injection, jailbreaks, or adversarial inputs designed to bypass safety filters and then use the resulting output in ways that would otherwise violate this AUP;
- use the Service to build tools or datasets intended to identify, track, or profile individuals based on protected characteristics;
- apply AI Output to consequential decisions about individuals (employment, lending, insurance, housing, immigration, benefits) without independent human review and compliance with applicable fairness and explainability requirements.

---

## 4. Security research

We welcome responsible security research. If you believe you have discovered a vulnerability:

- disclose it privately to **security@tryinspira.com**;
- do not access other users' data beyond what is minimally necessary to demonstrate the vulnerability;
- do not publish details until we have had a reasonable opportunity to remediate, typically 90 days or as otherwise agreed.

Good-faith research in line with this section will not be pursued under our AUP or Terms.

---

## 5. Content that requires extra care

Some content is not flatly prohibited but can raise legal or safety concerns. For these categories, you are responsible for confirming that your use is lawful and informed:

- medical, legal, financial, tax, mental-health, immigration, or safety-engineering advice — AI Output is not a substitute for qualified professionals;
- material relating to minors who are old enough to be users — keep safeguarding practices in mind;
- user-provided datasets that include personal data of third parties — you must have a lawful basis to process them.

---

## 6. Enforcement

When we suspect or confirm a violation, we may take any of the following actions, proportionate to the severity, history, and impact:

1. **Warning.** We notify you, explain the concern, and give you an opportunity to correct the behavior.
2. **Restriction.** We disable specific features, lower rate limits, or remove specific content while we investigate.
3. **Suspension.** We temporarily disable your Account while we investigate. Your data remains preserved.
4. **Termination.** We permanently terminate your Account under our [Terms of Service](./terms-of-service.md). Your data is handled per the [Privacy Policy](./privacy-policy.md).
5. **Legal referral.** Where the law requires or permits, we report to law enforcement, to NCMEC, or to affected third parties.

We may skip steps and move directly to suspension or termination in cases of serious or ongoing harm — for example, CSAM, coordinated attacks on the Service, credible threats of imminent violence, or legal orders.

---

## 7. Reporting violations

To report a violation of this AUP, email **abuse@tryinspira.com** *(alias to be configured)*. Include:

- a description of the conduct;
- links, URLs, project identifiers, or usernames, if known;
- any context that helps us act quickly;
- your contact information (optional, but helpful if we need to follow up).

We take every report seriously and keep reporter information confidential to the extent permitted by law. We may share reports with affected users, law enforcement, or other parties where necessary to resolve the matter.
